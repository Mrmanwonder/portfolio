# Systems & Endurance

## Chapter 02 --- Recognition

### Honors & Achievements Specification

Version 1.0

> Recognition is presented as earned momentum, not a list of awards. The
> medal is the protagonist of this chapter.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 1. Narrative Intent

The visitor should feel that achievements are the consequence of
consistent effort established in the Hero chapter.

The medal does not celebrate vanity. It introduces evidence.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 2. Visual Composition

Background: - Pure white - Minimal distractions - Editorial layout

Primary focal object: - One oversized 3D medal suspended by a ribbon.

Secondary content: - Achievement information revealed behind the medal.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 3. Medal Specification

Diameter: 220--280 px on desktop.

Material: - Physically based metallic gold - Brushed radial finish -
Micro scratches - Chamfered edge - Embossed center

Ribbon: - Deep crimson fabric - Slight weave texture - Cloth-like
bending - Visible thickness

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 4. Animation Timeline

00% --- Medal off-screen left.

15% --- Ribbon enters.

30% --- Medal swings into frame.

45% --- Overshoots center.

55% --- Settles while twisting.

65% --- Achievement is revealed as the medal passes.

85% --- Medal exits right.

100% --- Next achievement begins.

Transitions between achievements overlap slightly to maintain
continuity.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 5. Physics

The medal behaves as if suspended.

Requirements: - Gravity-influenced arc - Spring settling - Rotational
inertia - Ribbon lag - No robotic easing

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 6. Achievement Presentation

Each achievement occupies an editorial panel.

Panel contents: - Title - Date - Competition or institution - Short
description - Supporting statistic (optional) - Medal or badge icon

No generic card grids.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 7. Lighting

Soft daylight illumination.

Specular highlights travel across the medal during rotation.

Ribbon remains matte to contrast with the metal.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 8. Interaction

Hover: - Medal rotates a few degrees. - Reflection changes. - Ribbon
subtly reacts.

Scroll: - Drives the medal choreography only. - Text remains readable
throughout.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 9. Accessibility

Reduced Motion: - Medal fades into place. - Achievements crossfade. - No
swinging animation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 10. Performance

Maintain 60 FPS.

Use compressed textures.

Avoid expensive real-time reflections unless hardware allows.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 11. Acceptance Criteria

The visitor should remember: - the medal, - at least one achievement, -
and the transition into the Projects chapter.

Recognition should feel prestigious, understated and cinematic rather
than celebratory.
